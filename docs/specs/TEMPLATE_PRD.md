## 1. Problem / Opportunity
What problem are we solving, for whom, and why does it matter now (to the user and
the business)? One paragraph. If it doesn't make someone say "yes, that's worth
doing," tighten it.

## 2. Target users / personas
Who uses this. Their context, pain points, motivations.

## 3. Goals
What success looks like (outcomes, not features). Make them measurable.

## 4. Non-Goals
Explicitly out of scope. Non-goals prevent scope creep and surface trade-offs —
as important as goals.

## 5. Success metrics (KPIs)
How we'll measure success. Each metric ties to a goal. Include launch/exit criteria.

## 6. Requirements / Features (MoSCoW)
- **Must have** — ship-blockers.
- **Should have** — important, not blocking.
- **Could have** — nice-to-have.
- **Won't have (now)** — deferred.

## 7. Non-functional requirements
Performance/latency, scale, security, privacy/compliance, availability, cost.

## 8. User flows
Key journeys (numbered steps or a diagram).

## 9. Open questions
Unresolved decisions + who owns them.

## 10. Milestones / rollout
Phased delivery + exit criteria per phase.
