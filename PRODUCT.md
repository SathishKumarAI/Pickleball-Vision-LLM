# PRODUCT.md — Pickleball.Vision

## Register
**Split.** Landing/marketing surfaces = **brand** (design IS the product).
App surfaces (dashboard, upload, jobs, analyses, admin) = **product** (design
SERVES the task). Apply the matching register per surface; default = product.

## Target users
- **Recreational players** — want simple, motivating feedback on their game.
- **Competitive players & coaches** — want positioning, shot, and rally analytics.
- **Club/league admins** (later) — batch analysis, oversight, the admin log view.
Context: post-match, on desktop, uploading a phone-recorded clip and waiting a
couple minutes for results.

## Purpose
Turn an uploaded pickleball match video into an **annotated replay + a coaching
almanac** (court-aware analytics + plain-language feedback) in minutes — affordable,
automated game analysis for everyone.

## Brand personality
**Athletic · editorial · confident.** A vintage sports-almanac/poster sensibility
meets a modern AI tool. Tactile, printed, a little loud on the landing; precise and
legible in the app. Three words: *bold, sporting, crafted.*

## Anti-references
- Generic SaaS dark-glass + purple gradients on white.
- Timid, evenly-distributed pastel palettes; flat type scales.
- Inter/Roboto/Space-Grotesk "AI default" look.
- Over-animated micro-interaction confetti with no hierarchy.

## Strategic design principles
1. **Brand loud, product quiet.** The landing can shout (poster type, marquee,
   hard shadows); the app stays calm, legible, and fast.
2. **Print logic.** Hard ink borders, offset shadows, grain, editorial numbers —
   structure over decoration.
3. **One accent does the pointing.** Chartreuse = action/highlight; flame =
   labels/alerts. Never both for the same job.
4. **Legibility is non-negotiable** even when bold (see Accessibility).

## Accessibility
- **WCAG AA**: body ≥4.5:1, large/bold ≥3:1, placeholders ≥4.5:1. Verify accent-on-
  paper for any small text.
- **prefers-reduced-motion**: every animation (marquee, floaty, fadeup) has a
  reduced/instant alternative.
- Visible keyboard focus on all interactive elements; not color-only signaling.
